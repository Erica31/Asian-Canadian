Vicky Sunohara

Career & Achievements | Current & Coaching Life | Gallery

Vicky Sunohara is known as "The Wayne Gretzky of Woman's Hockey". She was a former ice hockey player, three-time olympic medallist, and currently a Canadian ice hockey coach at University of Toronto.

Career & Achievements

Vicky Sunohara began hockey at a very young age. Her late father, David Sunohara, played college ice hockey and was a hockey enthusiast. He would build a backyard rink in the winters and introduced skating to Vicky Sunohara at the age of two and a half. At the age of 5, Sunohara began playing on a boys hockey team but was eventually banned due to her gender. She ended up dominating every level of the girls competition.

After high school, she recieved a full athletic scholarship to Northeastern University in Boston, where she played 2 seasons. While playing for the Northeastern Huskies as a freshman, she led the team in scoring as they claimed the 1989 ECAC Hockey conference championship title. She again led the Huskies in scoring in the 1989-90 season, and was named the NCAA All-American team. Sunohara also played for the University of Toronto, where her team won the Ontario University Athletics women's ice hockey championships in 1990-91 and 1991-92.

Sunohara has represented Canada in many international competitions, including 3 olympics and many World championships. She won a silver medal at the Winter Olympics in 1998 Ngagano, Japan, and a gold medal in both Winter Olympics of 2002 and 2006, in Salt Lake City, Utah, and Turin, Italy. As part of the Canadian national team, she won 15 gold medals and 3 silver medals.
